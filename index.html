Coverage · 7 orgs: Niyo Global, Jupiter, UNI, OneCard, Cheq, Credilio (= Novio in Satori), and Kiwi (Gokiwi).
Booking source: Satori — flight_orders JOIN payments WHERE status='PAYMENT_SUCCESSFUL' across 6 orgs (deduplicated by order_id or userId). Kiwi has no Satori schema so its bookings (29 events / 24 unique) come from PostHog's PayConfirm_Success event.
Cancellations: A cancelled booking still counts — it had a successful payment. Refunds don't reduce the booking count. This is gross bookings, matching how the business tracks activity, not net-of-cancellations.
Aggregate user-days: Distinct (user, IST-day) pairs. A user who searches on 10 different days counts as 10; a user who searches 10 times in one day counts as 1. Fair gauge of repeated-visit demand.
Booking conversion: total bookings ÷ aggregate user-days. Numerator counts every booking (repeats included); denominator counts every distinct (user, day) search visit. Interpretation: "Of every 100 search-visit-days, ~5 produced a booking." Repeat bookings and repeat visits are both credited, giving a session-level yield rather than a pure people-through-the-funnel percentage.
